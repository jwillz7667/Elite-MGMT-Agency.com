Why multi-platform is the only safe model.

Every six months, a platform makes a policy decision that wipes out a category of creator businesses overnight. Reddit deletes a swath of NSFW subs. TikTok updates its community guidelines. Instagram quietly shadowbans tagged-adult accounts. Twitter changes hands and reverses every prior policy. The unifying lesson is that no single channel is permanent.

The creators who survive these cycles do not have a "best channel." They have a portfolio. New traffic flows from four to eight sources simultaneously, with content adapted to each platform's actual policies and audience. When one channel gets cut, the others backfill while we rebuild.

That is the strategic core of our marketing service: redundancy and adaptability above any single tactic. We run every channel that legitimately works for creators on the roster, and we maintain a documented playbook for each.

The second pillar — and the one most agencies skip entirely — is mainstream. Editorial features, podcast bookings, brand collaborations, mainstream press placements. The traffic these generate is smaller than Reddit on a per-day basis, but the brand asset compounds for five to ten years.

A creator with a feature in The Cut, an appearance on a top-50 podcast, and a brand partnership with a recognized DTC company has built equity that survives any single platform deprecation. Her name is in the public record. Her audience is qualified. New mainstream press becomes easier each cycle.

Most creators on the roster start with paid-platform-only marketing and graduate to a hybrid where mainstream features fund the next year's brand work. We design for that arc from day one. See the creator-economy view →

The channels we run.

01 · Reddit

Highest-converting channel for most roster creators. Subreddit-by-subreddit posting cadence, comment engagement, verified-account flairs, AMA scheduling, karma-building accounts. Routinely 25–40% of new subscriber traffic.

02 · Instagram

Top-of-funnel and brand asset. Reels, stories, grid, close-friends. Compliance reviewed weekly. Cross-funnel to the creator's link-in-bio routing into the right paid platform based on offer state.

03 · TikTok

Largest discovery surface. Lifestyle-led content that lives within community guidelines while signaling personality and aesthetic. Indirect funnel — never a direct platform mention.

04 · X (Twitter)

Direct-funnel-friendly. Most permissive policy among mainstream channels. Higher-frequency posting, scheduled threads, paid mention swaps with non-competing creators.

05 · Threads & Bluesky

Newer, less saturated. Important for younger creators establishing presence early. Daily posting; experimentation with how each platform handles adult-adjacent content.

06 · Paid acquisition

Where it's allowed. Reddit ads, niche programmatic networks, paid creator-to-creator shoutouts. We avoid spending on platforms that will ban the underlying account.

07 · SEO funnels

Compounding asset. Stage-name SEO, category SEO, evergreen blog content, structured data, schema markup. Slow but doesn't deprecate.

08 · Mainstream PR

Brand-equity engine. The Cut, Bustle, Vanity Fair, GQ, top-50 podcasts. Pitched through our partnership team, run through the New York editorial network.

Marketing FAQ.

What channels do you run for OnlyFans marketing?

Instagram, TikTok, Reddit, X (Twitter), Threads, Bluesky, paid acquisition (where platforms allow creator-economy advertising), Google Search via SEO funnels, mainstream PR through the New York editorial network, and creator-to-creator collaborations. We avoid Telegram-channel growth — too low-trust, too high-risk.

Do you do paid ads for OnlyFans?

Where permitted: Reddit ads, niche programmatic networks, and creator-to-creator paid shoutouts. Meta and Google reject most adult-creator advertising at the policy level, so we don't waste creator budget on accounts that will get banned. Where paid does work, it runs through compliant landing pages we operate.

How long until new traffic shows up?

Reddit funnels and X engagement show meaningful traffic inside 14 days. TikTok/Instagram pull-through takes 30–60 days depending on account history. SEO funnels are 90+ days. Mainstream PR is unpredictable but compounds — a single major feature can outperform a year of organic.

Will my mainstream accounts get banned?

Not if they're run within platform TOS. Our content team is trained on each platform's actual policies — not on the rumor mill — and our compliance lead reviews every account weekly. Where a platform's policy genuinely conflicts with monetization, we redesign the funnel rather than risk a ban.

Do you have a Reddit playbook?

Yes. Reddit is the highest-converting traffic source for most paid-platform creators. We run subreddit-by-subreddit posting cadence, comment engagement, AMA scheduling, karma-building accounts, and verification posts. Reddit alone routinely accounts for 25–40% of new subscriber traffic on our roster.

What about SEO?

Two layers: SEO for the creator's stage-name (rank for her name and search variants — protect against scammers), and SEO for category terms where the creator wants discovery. Both are compounding assets that survive any single platform's algorithm shift.

How does mainstream PR work for OnlyFans creators?

Our partnerships team has placed roster creators in The Cut, Bustle, Vanity Fair, GQ and podcast circuits including The Cut Podcast, Call Her Daddy, and assorted New York radio. The pitch is rarely about the platform — it's about the creator's actual story. Mainstream features compound over five-year horizons.

What's the marketing commission?

Marketing is included in the agency revenue share — no separate marketing retainer, no separate ad budget markup, no separate PR fee. Paid ad spend (when used) is the creator's, billed at cost. Brand deals are 15–20% commission on deal value.
