Privacy Policy.

1. Scope and applicability

This policy applies to (a) visitors to our website, (b) applicants to our creator roster who submit information via the application form, (c) creators under contract with Elite MGMT, (d) job applicants and contractors, and (e) people who contact us by email, phone, or any other channel.

It does not apply to third-party platforms a creator is operating on (e.g., OnlyFans, Fansly, Instagram). Those platforms have their own privacy policies which govern the data they collect directly from you.

For EU/UK residents, our representative under GDPR Art. 27 can be contacted at the same email address. We do not have an EU establishment; the representative is appointed solely for receiving inquiries from supervisory authorities and data subjects.

3. Information we collect

3.1 Information you give us

  • Application data: stage name, legal name (only if you choose to share it during diligence), age verification, social handles, current platforms and revenue, country/state, time-zone, and a free-form description of what you want from the partnership.
  • Onboarding data (creators only): identity documents (government-issued photo ID, model release, IRS Form W-9 / W-8BEN), banking information for payouts, and the contracts you sign with us.
  • Operational data (creators only): the content and messaging assets you provide for production, captions, scheduling preferences, voice samples for chat training, and the ongoing operational metadata generated by working together.
  • Communications: emails, calls, recorded discovery calls (with consent), and any messages you send to us through any channel.

3.2 Information we collect automatically

  • Server logs: IP address, user-agent, referrer, request path, response code, and timestamp. Retained 30 days for security and operations.
  • Analytics events: page views, link clicks, scroll depth, and screen size — aggregated by a privacy-first analytics platform that does not set cross-site cookies or fingerprint visitors.
  • Cookies: see Section 14 and our cookie policy.

3.3 Information we receive from third parties

  • Background checks (chat-team applicants only): from accredited background-check vendors with the applicant's explicit consent.
  • Public information: publicly accessible social profiles linked from your application; verification information from the platforms you operate on (only what is shared publicly).

3.4 Information we do not collect

  • Government identity numbers (SSN, passport number) from website visitors or applicants. We collect tax identification only at the point of onboarding for payout purposes and only from creators we have signed.
  • Real-time location data. We use coarse geolocation derived from IP address solely for analytics and security.
  • Information about people other than the data subject (we do not buy data, scrape it, or accept it from third-party data brokers).

4. How and why we use information

We use personal information only for the purposes set out below:

  • Roster intake: reviewing applications, conducting diligence calls, and deciding whether to sign a creator.
  • Service delivery: operating the contracted services (content production, chat operations, marketing, DMCA enforcement, brand and concierge) on behalf of signed creators.
  • Payments: processing revenue share, issuing 1099s/W-8 forms, and handling banking for creators on payout schedules.
  • Compliance: US 18 USC §2257 record-keeping where applicable (see 2257 statement), tax reporting, KYC and anti-money-laundering checks on payouts, and responding to lawful legal process.
  • Security: detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, account takeover, and unauthorized scraping of the website.
  • Communications: responding to your inquiries and operational messages, and (only where you have opted in) sending the journal newsletter.
  • Analytics & product improvement: understanding aggregated traffic patterns to improve the website. We do not perform individual-level profiling for advertising.

5. Legal bases (GDPR Art. 6)

For EU/UK data subjects, the legal bases on which we process personal data are:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): operating contracted services for signed creators; processing onboarding and payout information.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): reviewing roster applications you submit to us, securing the website, preventing fraud, and analyzing aggregated traffic. We balance these interests against your rights and have determined they do not override them; you may object to this processing at any time.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): tax reporting and 18 USC §2257 record-keeping requirements.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): non-essential cookies, the journal newsletter, recording discovery calls. Consent can be withdrawn at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

6. Sharing & disclosure

We do not sell personal information. We do not share it for advertising, data brokerage, or any analogous purpose.

We disclose personal information only:

  • To sub-processors who provide services to us under contract (Section 7).
  • To banking, payments and tax advisors as strictly required to process payouts and file returns on behalf of signed creators.
  • To legal counsel as required for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.
  • To law enforcement or courts in response to a valid legal demand. We will challenge any demand we believe is overbroad and notify the data subject to the extent permitted by law.
  • In connection with a corporate transaction (merger, acquisition, sale of assets) where the recipient is bound to honor this policy.

7. Sub-processors

The current sub-processors that handle personal information on our behalf are:

  • Cloudflare, Inc. (US/global) — CDN, DDoS protection, edge security.
  • Amazon Web Services, Inc. (US, us-east-1, eu-west-2) — primary application hosting and encrypted storage for operational documents.
  • Plaintext (1Password Business) — secret management for creator banking and platform credentials.
  • Plausible Insights OÜ (EU) — privacy-first analytics; no cookies, no cross-site tracking.
  • Stripe, Inc. (US) — payment processing for retainers, where applicable.
  • Mercury Technologies, Inc. (US) — banking and ACH for creator payouts.
  • Postmark / ActiveCampaign (US) — transactional email and (opt-in) newsletter delivery.
  • Vanta, Inc. (US) — compliance monitoring and audit log retention.

A current list of sub-processors is available on request to privacy@elite-mgmt-agency.com. We give creators 30 days' written notice of new sub-processors before they begin handling that creator's data, except where the change is required by law.

8. International transfers

We are headquartered in the United States and most of our processing happens there. Where we transfer personal data from the EEA, UK or Switzerland to the US (or to any third country not subject to a European Commission adequacy decision), we rely on the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914) with the UK Addendum where applicable, supplemented by additional technical and organizational measures (encryption in transit and at rest, role-based access control, audit logging).

You may request a copy of the relevant transfer mechanism by emailing the privacy contact above.

9. Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Application data from applicants we do not sign: deleted 90 days after the decision, unless you ask us to delete it sooner.
  • Operational data for signed creators: retained for the duration of the engagement plus the period required by applicable tax, anti-money-laundering and 18 USC §2257 record-keeping laws (currently a minimum of 7 years from the last applicable record).
  • Banking and payout records: 7 years after the final payment (US tax law).
  • Server logs: 30 days.
  • Analytics: aggregated indefinitely; raw events 90 days.

10. Security

We maintain administrative, technical and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure or destruction. These include:

  • TLS 1.3 in transit; AES-256 at rest for all stored documents and credentials.
  • Role-based access control with the principle of least privilege; multi-factor authentication on every employee account.
  • Annual third-party penetration testing; quarterly internal access reviews.
  • SOC 2 Type II monitoring through Vanta.
  • A documented incident-response plan with a 72-hour breach-notification commitment to data subjects and supervisory authorities where required.

No security program is perfect. Should we ever experience a breach affecting your personal data, we will notify you and, where required by law, the relevant supervisory authority.

11. Your rights (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are in the EEA, UK or Switzerland you have the following rights:

  • Access: obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure (right to be forgotten): request deletion subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Restriction of processing.
  • Data portability: receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing.
  • Withdraw consent at any time for processing based on consent.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In the UK, this is the Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk).

To exercise any right, email privacy@elite-mgmt-agency.com. We respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before disclosing data — we will not require more information than is reasonable.

12. Your rights (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident:

  • Right to know what personal information we have collected about you, the sources, the purposes, and the categories of third parties with which we shared it.
  • Right to delete personal information (subject to legal retention obligations).
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, so this right is exercised by default.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information.
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights.

To exercise any right, email privacy@elite-mgmt-agency.com with the subject line "CCPA request". We respond within 45 days. We do not require account creation to submit a request.

13. Children

The Elite MGMT website and all of its services are intended exclusively for adults 18 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If we discover that we have collected such information, we will delete it immediately. If you believe we may have such information, please contact privacy@elite-mgmt-agency.com.

14. Cookies & similar technologies

We use strictly-necessary cookies for site security and session continuity, and a privacy-first analytics tool that does not set tracking cookies. We do not use advertising cookies and we do not let third parties drop cookies on our pages.

The full list, purposes and durations are documented in our cookie policy.

15. Do Not Track & Global Privacy Control

Our website respects the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal. When your browser sends GPC, we treat it as a verifiable request to opt out of sale or sharing under CCPA. We do not respond to legacy Do Not Track (DNT) headers because the standard was deprecated, but our default behavior is equivalent: no cross-site tracking, no advertising cookies.

16. Changes to this policy

We will update this policy when our practices change, when sub-processors change materially, or when applicable law changes. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent change. For material changes that affect signed creators, we will also notify you directly by email and give you 30 days' notice before the change takes effect.
