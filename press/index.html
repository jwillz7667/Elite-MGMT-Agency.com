Press & media.

Elite MGMT is the New York women-only creator management agency operating the careers of under-50 top-tier creators across OnlyFans, Fansly and the broader creator economy. We talk to credentialed journalists writing on the creator economy, platform policy, women in tech, and the business of independent media. Bios, photography, logos and the agency fact sheet are below.

Topics we speak on.

We are happy to provide on-record comment, background, or expert sourcing for stories on:

  • The OnlyFans / Fansly / creator-economy business model and economics.
  • Women in independent media and platform-based careers.
  • Platform policy — payment processing risk, KYC/AML, age verification, US 18 USC §2257.
  • Creator privacy & safety: stalking, doxxing, NCII, DMCA enforcement.
  • Taxation, banking and back-office for self-employed creators.
  • The downsides of offshored "chatter" operations and why we don't use them.

We will not, on or off the record, identify creators on our roster who have not publicly chosen to be identified. Requests for creator-direct interviews are routed via our press office.

Founder bios.

Mariana Vega, Co-founder & Managing Partner

Mariana co-founded Elite MGMT in 2019 after seven years operating top accounts at a competitor and watching offshored chat operations destroy creator income across the industry. At Elite MGMT she leads the day-to-day operating business: roster strategy, chat operations, and the production studio.

Before Elite MGMT, Mariana was Head of Operations at Reign Media (acq. 2018) and managed digital strategy for two Top-100 OnlyFans creators between 2015 and 2018. Mariana is a graduate of NYU Stern (BS, Finance, 2014).

Mariana is on the record for stories about chat-ops, retention, and the economics of the roster. Available via the press office.

Sasha Pham, Co-founder & Head of Marketing

Sasha co-founded Elite MGMT after running growth and brand at two consumer-tech startups including Niche (acq. Twitter, 2015). At Elite MGMT she heads marketing, growth and brand — the function that brings new subscribers to creators on the roster and converts top creators into mainstream-recognizable names.

Sasha holds an MBA from Columbia Business School (2017) and a BA in Comparative Literature from Yale (2012). She has been quoted in The New York Times, The Information, WIRED and on the Acquired podcast about the creator economy.

Sasha is on the record for stories about marketing portfolios, the platform-vs-platform economics, and the convergence of the creator economy with the mainstream.

Press quotes on the record.

The number of OnlyFans creators making over $250k a year has roughly doubled since 2022 — and almost none of that growth is on the platform. It's in chat and in marketing. The platform is the storefront. The business is everywhere else.Mariana Vega, Co-founder
The agencies that win in 2026 are the ones that treat creator businesses like venture-backed startups — multi-channel, multi-platform, defensible, and built around an actual human being who can leave the industry on her own terms.Sasha Pham, Co-founder
Offshored chat operations are the single most-destructive practice in this industry. They erode trust, they erode revenue, and the creator pays the bill for both.Mariana Vega, Co-founder
We say no to about 99% of the applications we get. Not because the creators aren't good — many of them are great — but because we can only properly run a few dozen businesses at a time without compromising quality.Sasha Pham, Co-founder
