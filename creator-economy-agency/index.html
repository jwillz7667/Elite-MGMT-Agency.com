The economy around the content.

"Creator economy" started as a Silicon Valley term and has become the actual job description for a generation of women earning seven and eight figures from content. The unit of analysis is no longer a single platform; it's a portfolio.

A typical creator on our roster will, in any given year, run: a paywalled platform business worth $1–4M, an Instagram/TikTok presence funneling new traffic, four to eight branded partnerships, two live events, a merch drop, and a small public-equity portfolio of the DTC brands she's helped to grow. The job of an agency is to make all of that legible and operable, with one team you can call.

Most agencies serve one of those lines. A talent agency books brand deals. An OnlyFans agency runs the inbox. A management firm handles touring. Each takes a cut. Each has its own bookkeeping, its own contracts, its own contradictions. The fragmentation is the largest tax on a creator's revenue.

Elite MGMT collapses that. One contract. One partner. One brand identity. One team that handles content, chat, marketing, brand work, legal, safety, finance and the long-term plan for what happens when content slows down.

Crucially, we operate the paywalled-platform side as the engine — OnlyFans and Fansly are typically the largest single revenue line — and we use it to fund and de-risk the mainstream and brand work that compounds over five and ten year horizons. Subscription cash flow is the most undervalued asset a creator can have. We treat it as such.

The output is a roster of women who don't talk about "going viral." They talk about retention curves, ARPU, LTV-to-CAC, brand-deal pipeline, and how to structure equity in the DTC company they're advising. That's the creator economy.

The revenue stack we run.

01 · Paywalled platforms

OnlyFans, Fansly, Loyalfans, custom subscription sites. The engine — recurring monthly revenue with high gross margin. Typically 55–75% of total creator revenue. See the OnlyFans page →

02 · Mainstream social

Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, Threads. Treated as the top of the funnel and as standalone revenue (creator fund, partnerships, ads where applicable). Editorial calendar, post production and analytics in-house.

03 · Brand & partnerships

80+ active brand partners. End-to-end: brief, creative, contract negotiation, FTC compliance, production, posting, attribution, escrow. 15–20% commission on deal value.

04 · Live events & touring

Curated events, meet-and-greets, panel appearances, retreats, brand activations. Venue, ticketing, security, travel — handled. 10% commission on net.

05 · Merchandise & product

Limited drops, evergreen lines, capsule collections, licensed product. Design, manufacturing, fulfillment, CX. Run through a creator-owned LLC; the agency operates, the creator owns.

06 · Equity & advisory

Angel checks into DTC brands creators champion. Advisory roles. Equity-for-IP licensing. Founder roles in startups creators incubate with us. Structured by our finance partner.

Questions we get.

What is a creator economy agency?

A creator economy agency manages the full revenue stack for a content creator across multiple platforms — paid subscriptions, brand partnerships, merchandise, live events, equity in startups, and traditional media. It is broader than an OnlyFans agency or a talent agency; the goal is durable, multi-stream revenue rather than dependence on any one platform.

Who is this for?

Women creators with at least one substantial revenue stream — OnlyFans, Fansly, YouTube, Twitch, TikTok, Substack, an Etsy shop, a private community — who want to systematically build the other streams without overextending their time on any one platform.

Do you work with mainstream creators?

Yes. About 30% of the roster runs mainstream-first content (fitness, lifestyle, food, fashion) with paid platforms as a secondary revenue stream. The model works in both directions — we help paid-platform creators move mainstream, and we help mainstream creators monetize without compromising their brand.

What about brand deals and partnerships?

Brand work is run in-house by the partnership team. Standard process: brand brief, creator approval, contract negotiation, deliverable production, FTC compliance review, payment escrow. We have an active roster of 80+ brand partners ranging from DTC to mainstream apparel to fintech.

How is this different from an influencer agency?

Most influencer agencies are pure broker shops — they pitch creators to brands for a commission on the deal. We do the brand work plus run the underlying content business: production, chat, marketing, safety, legal. The combination is what makes the multi-platform strategy actually executable.

What about exit / equity / cash-out?

Creator equity is a real category now — angel deals, advisor roles, equity-for-IP licensing, and creator-led product launches. Our finance team works with creators on building outside-the-content revenue streams: index funds, real estate, equity in DTC brands they champion, founder roles in startups they advise.

Do you take creators who aren't on OnlyFans?

Yes — many. The criteria is signal and ambition, not platform mix. We run creators whose primary revenue is Twitch, YouTube, Substack, or live touring. The shared thread is that they want a real agency apparatus, not just a manager.

What's the commission structure for non-paid-platform revenue?

Tiered by revenue stream: paid-platform revenue (OnlyFans, Fansly, etc.) is on the standard revenue share; brand deals are typically 15–20% commission; live events and merchandise are 10%; equity and advisory work is structured separately. Specifics on a discovery call.
